What is a Pinhole Camera?

A pinhole camera is a simple optical device that captures images without using a lens. It consists of a dark box with a tiny hole on one side. Light from an external scene passes through this hole and projects an inverted image onto the opposite surface inside the box.

The smaller the hole, the sharper the image—but less light enters, making it dimmer. This principle is the foundation of how cameras work today, even digital ones.

How It Works

  • Light rays travel in straight lines.
  • Only a few rays pass through the pinhole, forming an inverted image.
  • The image can be projected onto a surface or recorded digitally.
